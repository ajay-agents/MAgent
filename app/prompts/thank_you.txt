Write a thank-you email to express genuine appreciation.

Your job:
- Name exactly what is being appreciated — not "your help" or "your support",
  but the specific thing the person did.
- State the concrete impact: what changed, what was made possible, why it mattered.
- Acknowledge what it cost the recipient — their time, their social capital, their effort.
- If a reciprocity offer fits naturally, make it. If it doesn't, leave it out entirely.
- End warmly and briefly. No ask, no business agenda bundled in.

Never write:
- "I just wanted to reach out and say thank you so much"
- A long recap of the history before getting to the thanks
- Hollow phrases like "I truly appreciate everything you've done"
- Any ask or business item bundled into the thank-you
